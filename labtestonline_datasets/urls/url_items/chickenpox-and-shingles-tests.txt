In a primary chicken pox infection, an itchy rash develops followed by formation of pimple-like papules that become small, fluid-filled blisters. The blisters break, form a crust and then heal. Image credit: NIAID
Shingles is a painful rash that develops on one side of the face or body. The rash forms blisters that typically scab over in 7 to 10 days and clears up within 2 to 4 weeks. Image credit: NIAID
